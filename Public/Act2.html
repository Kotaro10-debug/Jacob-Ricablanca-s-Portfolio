jacob's Chicken Kare-Kare Recipe

A.Ingredients and Measurements

Meat:

  • 1 1/2kg "Chicken", cut into serving pieces (preferably thigh, leg, and wing parts for richer flavor).

Vegetables:

  • 1 cup "banana heart"(Puso ng Saging), sliced into dice pieces.
  • 1 bundle "pechay"(bok choy).
  • 1 bundle "string beans"(sitaw), cut into 2-inch lengths.
  • 1 cup "eggplant", sliced.

Kare-Kare Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup "ground roased peanuts"(or 1/2 cup natural peanut butter).
  • 3 tbsp annatto seeds.
  • 1/4 cup "cooking oil".
  • 1 medium "onion", finely chopped.
  • 4 cloves "garlic", minced.
  • 4 cups "chicken broth".
  • 2 tbsp "rice flour".
  • Salt to taste.

For Serving:

  • 1/2 cup "bagoong alamang"(fermented shrimp paste).

B. Cooking Tools Needed:

  • Large Pot.
  • Frying Pan/kawali.
  • Strainer.
  • Wooden spoon or ladle.
  • Knife and chopping board.
  • Measuring cups and spoons.
  • Blender or mortar and pestle(for peanuts).

C. Step-by-Step Cooking Procedure:

  1. Step 1: Boil the Chicken.
    • Place chicken in a pot and add enough water to cover.
    • Boil for "30-40 minutes" until tender.
    • Remove chicken and " reserve 4 cups of the broth".
  2. Step 2: Prepare Annatto Oil.
    • Heat oil over low heat.
    • Add annatto seeds and simmer until oil turns orange-red.
    • Strain and discard seeds. Set oil aside.
  3. Step 3: Make the Peanut Sauce.
    • In a pan, heat annatto oil.
    • Saute garlic and onion until fragrant.
    • Add ground peanuts or peanut butter; stir continuously.
    • Pour in the chicken broth gradually.
    • Dissolve rice flour in 1/2 cup water, then add to the sauce.
    • Simmer until thick and smooth.
  4. Step 4: Combine Chicken and Sauce.
    • Add boiled chicken to the peanut sauce.
    • Simmer for "10-15 minutes" to absorb flavor.
    • Season with salt to taste.
  5. Step 5: Cook the Vegetables.
    • Banan heart: 5 minutes.
    • Eggplant: 3 minutes.
    • String beans and peachay: 2 minutes.

D. Serving

  • Serve hot with "steamed white rice"
  • Paired with "bagoong alamang"(fermented shrimp paste).
